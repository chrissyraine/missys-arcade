Our Story

More Than a Diner

For 25 years, Missy's Arcade Restaurant has been the heartbeat of Titusville — the kind of place where your coffee cup never runs empty and your waitress remembers your name.

The Beginning
Born and raised in Titusville, Missy Wescoat opened the doors on March 5, 2001, at just 29 years old. She brought her restaurant roots with her, starting her career at The Skillet inside True Value Hardware. What she built is something bigger than a diner — it's a gathering place.

The Vibe
Coca-Cola memorabilia. Vintage neon. Bright pink everywhere. Fun sayings like "Mind your own biscuits and life will be gravy." The signature interior is pure Missy — a time capsule of nostalgia and warmth.

The Rebuild
In 2018, a basement fire nearly took everything. Nine fire departments saved the block. Missy rebuilt — every pink wall, every Coke sign, every booth. Because Titusville needed its bright spot back.

The Family
Missy raised two boys here. Watched them grow up in the booths, around the counter, surrounded by community. It's a legacy in progress.

"What I love most about the diner vibe is that it's people from every walk of life, they all mesh together. These people are truly my family. That's how it's been my whole life."
