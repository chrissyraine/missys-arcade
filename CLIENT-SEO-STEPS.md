# Missy's Arcade — SEO Setup Guide

**Client-facing documentation for search engine optimization**

This guide walks you through the essential steps to ensure Missy's Arcade shows up in Google and Bing search results, appears on Google Maps, and maintains strong online visibility.

---

## Part 1: Google Search Console Setup

Google Search Console is the direct line between your site and Google. It lets you submit your sitemap, monitor search performance, and fix indexing issues.

### Step 1: Add Your Domain Property

1. Go to **[Google Search Console](https://search.google.com/search-console/about)**
2. Sign in with your Google Account (or create one)
3. Click **"Add Property"** (upper left)
4. Select **"Domain"** as the property type
5. Enter: `missysarcade.com` (without www)
6. Click **"Continue"**

### Step 2: Verify Domain Ownership via DNS

Google will ask you to prove you own the domain. You'll do this by adding a DNS TXT record.

**What to do:**

1. Google will show you a TXT record that looks like:
   ```
   google-site-verification=abc123def456...
   ```

2. Go to your domain registrar (the company where you bought missysarcade.com):
   - GoDaddy
   - Namecheap
   - Domain.com
   - Your registrar's website

3. Find the **DNS Settings** or **DNS Records** section

4. Add a new **TXT record** with:
   - **Name:** `@` or leave blank (depending on your registrar)
   - **Type:** `TXT`
   - **Value:** The verification code Google gave you

5. Save the record and return to Google Search Console

6. Click **"Verify"** in Google Search Console

**This typically takes 10 minutes to 48 hours to complete.** Google is checking the DNS to confirm you own the domain.

### Step 3: Submit Your Sitemap

Once verified:

1. In Google Search Console, go to **"Sitemaps"** (left menu)
2. Enter the sitemap URL: `missysarcade.com/sitemap.xml`
3. Click **"Submit"**

Your sitemap tells Google exactly which pages exist on your site and when they were last updated. This ensures all your content gets indexed quickly.

### Step 4: Monitor Search Performance (Monthly)

Once your site is indexed, you can track performance in Google Search Console:

- **Click-through Rate (CTR):** % of people who see you in search results and click
- **Impressions:** How many times your site appeared in search results
- **Average Position:** Where you rank (1 = top spot)
- **Queries:** What people searched for to find you

**Good targets after 3–6 months:**
- Impressions: 500–2,000/month
- Clicks: 50–200/month
- Average position: Top 10 for local searches like "diner near Titusville PA"

## Part 2: Google Business Profile Setup

Your Google Business Profile is **the most important tool for local restaurants**. It appears in Google Maps and local search results, and lets customers call, visit, and leave reviews.

### Step 1: Create Your Business Profile

1. Go to **[Google Business Profile](https://business.google.com)**
2. Sign in with your Google Account
3. Click **"Manage Now"** or **"Get Started"**
4. Search for your business: `Missy's Arcade`
5. If found, claim it. If not, click **"Create a new business"**

### Step 2: Complete All Business Information

Fill in these sections completely:

**Business Details:**
- Name: `Missy's Arcade`
- Address: `116 Diamond Street, Titusville, PA 16354`
- Phone: `(814) 827-8110`
- Website: `https://www.missysarcade.com`

**Business Category:**
- Primary: `Restaurant`
- Secondary: `American Cuisine, Breakfast/Brunch, Diner`

**Hours of Operation:**
- Monday–Saturday: 6:00 AM – 3:00 PM
- Sunday: 7:00 AM – 2:00 PM

**Service Area:**
- Select "Service area" if you do delivery
- Add: Titusville, Oil City, Rouseville (nearby communities)

**Attributes:**
- Check: Dine-in, Takeout, Casual dining, Parking available

### Step 3: Add Photos & Videos

Photos are crucial—they influence whether people visit.

**What to upload:**

1. **Hero shot:** Best photo of the restaurant exterior or dining room (1 photo)
2. **Signature dishes:** 3–5 photos of your most popular meals (hotdog, grilled cheese, fish fry, etc.)
3. **Dining experience:** 2–3 photos of the interior, counter, booths
4. **Team photo:** Optional—photo of you, staff, or the owner adds trust

**Best practices:**
- Use high-quality, well-lit photos
- Show real food and real people
- Update 1–2 new photos monthly (keeps your profile fresh)

### Step 4: Encourage Reviews

Reviews are the #1 way to build trust and rank higher in Google Maps.

**How to ask for reviews:**

1. Include your Google Maps link on your receipt or website
2. Ask customers in person: *"If you enjoyed your visit, we'd love a review on Google"*
3. Share the link on Facebook, your website, and email newsletters
4. **Never pay for reviews or ask friends/family to fake reviews**—Google will penalize you

**Where customers leave reviews:**
- Google Business Profile (most important)
- Yelp
- Facebook
- TripAdvisor

**Respond to every review** (within 24–48 hours):
- **Thank positive reviewers** for their support
- **Address negative reviews professionally** and offer to fix the issue offline

Good review volume:
- Target: 10–15 reviews in the first 3 months
- 30+ reviews = strong local authority
- Average rating: 4.5+ stars

## Part 3: Bing Webmaster Tools

Bing powers Bing search and Yahoo. It's smaller than Google but worth setting up.

### Step 1: Add Your Site

1. Go to **[Bing Webmaster Tools](https://www.bing.com/webmasters)**
2. Sign in (Microsoft Account)
3. Click **"Add a site"**
4. Enter: `https://www.missysarcade.com`

### Step 2: Verify Ownership

Choose one method:
- **Add XML file:** Download a file, upload to your web host
- **Add meta tag:** Add one line to your website's HTML (already done)
- **Add DNS record:** Similar to Google Search Console

### Step 3: Submit Sitemap

In Bing Webmaster Tools:
1. Go to **"Sitemaps"**
2. Add: `https://www.missysarcade.com/sitemap.xml`

Bing has fewer search queries than Google, but it's still worth maintaining.

## Part 4: What to Expect—Timeline & Metrics

### First 2 Weeks: Indexing Phase
- Google/Bing are crawling your site
- You may not see search traffic yet
- Focus on completing Google Business Profile

### Weeks 3–8: Early Visibility
- Your homepage begins ranking for branded searches (e.g., "Missy's Arcade")
- Local searches like "diner Titusville" may show your profile
- Expect 5–20 clicks/month from search

### Months 3–6: Growth Phase
- Ranking improves for local searches: "diner near me," "breakfast Titusville"
- Google Business Profile gets more visibility
- Expect 50–200 clicks/month

### 6+ Months: Authority Phase
- Reviews accumulate; ranking strengthens
- You rank for competitive terms
- Expect 200–500+ clicks/month depending on local competition

### Key Metrics to Track (Monthly)

**In Google Search Console:**
- Impressions (target: 500+)
- Clicks (target: 50+)
- Average ranking position (target: top 10 for local searches)

**In Google Business Profile:**
- Views (target: 100+ per month)
- Directions clicks (target: 20+ per month)
- Phone calls (track manually)
- Website clicks (target: 10+ per month)

**On Google Reviews:**
- New reviews per month (target: 3–5)
- Average rating (target: 4.5+)
